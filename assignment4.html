Challenge 5: Accessibility Statement

The accessibility on my website is moderately good. On the positive side, my html is laid out in the desired order (left to right top to bottom) for screen readers to make sense of and the heading structure is correct. It also has good text contrast and does not make use of javascript or non html content. Getting to the negative stuff, the chessboard will make absolutely no sense to a screen reader and there is no alt text available for it. Additionally the white chess pieces get washed out against the dark squares and it is very difficult to tell whether they are white or black. The black on black looks even more washed out.
